Alfredo Sauce

Description

Alfredo sauce is a creamy, rich Italian sauce made primarily of butter, heavy cream, and Parmesan cheese. It's known for its smooth, velvety texture and indulgent flavor. To make it, butter is melted in a pan, then heavy cream is added and simmered until it thickens. Grated Parmesan cheese is stirred in until it melts, creating a luscious, cheesy sauce. Garlic, salt, and pepper are often used to season it, and some recipes also include a touch of nutmeg for warmth.

Alfredo sauce is typically served with pasta, especially fettuccine, creating the classic dish Fettuccine Alfredo. It can also be used as a base for other dishes, like chicken Alfredo or shrimp Alfredo, and is often topped with fresh herbs or additional grated cheese. Its creamy, cheesy flavor is a favorite comfort food for many!

How to make homemade Alfredo Sauce

You'll need just five simple ingredients to make this basic Alfredo sauce recipe

Ingredients:

  • ¼ cup butter
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 ½ cups freshly grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 clove garlic, crushed
  • ¼ cup chopped fresh parsley

Steps:

  1. Gather all ingredients.
  2. Melt butter in a medium saucepan over low heat. Stir in cream and simmer for 5 minutes.
  3. Add cheese and garlic; whisk continuously until heated through. Stir in parsley.
  4. Serve immediately and enjoy!
